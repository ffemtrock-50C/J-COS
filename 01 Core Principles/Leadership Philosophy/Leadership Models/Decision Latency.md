# Decision Latency

## Principle

A leader should always be prepared to make a decision immediately, but should intentionally use whatever time is available to improve the quality of that decision without creating unacceptable operational risk.

---

## Key Sentences

"A good leader is capable of making the decision immediately. A great leader knows when delaying the decision will improve the decision without materially increasing risk."

"The environment determines the maximum allowable decision latency."

"Readiness is not the same as urgency."

"I stand ready to decide at time zero."

---

## Why it Matters

Decisiveness is often confused with speed.

Good leaders are decisive.

Great leaders understand when additional information is likely to change the decision.

---

## Examples

• Structure fire
Immediate decision.

• Personnel investigation
Intentional delay.

• Capital purchase
Maximum information gathering.

---

## Questions

What don't I know today that, if I knew tomorrow, would change this decision?
