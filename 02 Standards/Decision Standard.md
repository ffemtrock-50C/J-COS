# Decision Standard

**Version:** 0.1

## Purpose

This standard defines the decision-making process used throughout J-COS.

Its purpose is not to eliminate judgment, but to improve it by ensuring that important decisions are deliberate, evidence-based, transparent, and reviewable.

A good decision may still produce a poor outcome due to uncertainty. Conversely, a poor decision may occasionally produce a good outcome. J-COS evaluates the quality of the decision process, not solely the result.

---

# Decision Principles

Every significant decision should strive to:

- Clearly define the problem before proposing solutions.
- Distinguish facts from assumptions.
- Identify what is unknown.
- Seek evidence before opinions.
- Consider multiple reasonable alternatives.
- Evaluate second-order consequences.
- Document the reasoning behind the decision.
- Identify conditions that would warrant revisiting the decision.
- Capture lessons learned after implementation.

---

# Decision Workflow

Every significant decision follows these steps.

## 1. Define the Problem

What decision must be made?

Why is it important?

What happens if no decision is made?

---

## 2. Gather Facts

What do we know?

What evidence supports those facts?

What information is missing?

---

## 3. Separate Facts from Assumptions

Facts are supported by evidence.

Assumptions are beliefs awaiting confirmation.

Both are valuable.

They should never be confused.

---

## 4. Identify Stakeholders

Who is affected?

Who owns the decision?

Who should provide input?

Who must be informed?

---

## 5. Generate Alternatives

Avoid false choices.

Develop multiple reasonable courses of action.

Document advantages and disadvantages of each.

---

## 6. Evaluate Risks

Operational

Financial

Legal

Political

Personnel

Reputational

Strategic

Public Safety

---

## 7. Make the Decision

Document:

Decision

Reasoning

Evidence supporting the decision

Confidence level

Remaining uncertainty

---

## 8. Communicate

Who needs to know?

What do they need to understand?

How will success be measured?

---

## 9. Review

What actually happened?

Were our assumptions correct?

Would we make the same decision again?

What should become institutional knowledge?

---

# Decision Record

Every major decision should be capable of being summarized in one page.

The decision record should include:

- Problem
- Facts
- Assumptions
- Unknowns
- Stakeholders
- Options Considered
- Decision
- Rationale
- Risks
- Review Trigger
- Lessons Learned

---

# Standard of Evidence

The strength of a decision should be proportional to the quality of the available evidence.

When evidence is limited, uncertainty should be acknowledged rather than ignored.

J-COS encourages confidence with humility.

Leaders should be willing to change a decision when new evidence materially alters the situation.

---

# Core Philosophy

The objective is not to make perfect decisions.

The objective is to consistently make the best decision possible using the information available at the time.
